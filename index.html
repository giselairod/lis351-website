El Yunque: The Only Tropical Rainforest in the U.S.

Explore lush trails, waterfalls, and wildlife in Puerto Rico’s national rainforest.

Plan your visit to El Yunque

Features

Unique Rainforest Climate

El Yunque receives heavy rainfall year-round, creating lush vegetation, rivers, and waterfalls throughout the forest.

Native Wildlife

The forest is home to coquí frogs, rare birds, and plant species found nowhere else in the world.

Hiking & Waterfalls

Visitors can hike scenic mountain trails and swim near natural waterfalls surrounded by tropical greenery.

Featured Content

El Yunque National Forest spans over 28,000 acres of protected land. It plays a crucial role in Puerto Rico’s ecosystem by providing fresh water, preserving biodiversity, and supporting tourism.

Plan Your Visit

Best Time to Visit

Mornings are less crowded and offer clearer views of the mountains. Rain showers are common throughout the day.

What to Bring

Wear grip shoes and bring water, bug spray, and a rain jacket to stay safe and comfortable while hiking.
